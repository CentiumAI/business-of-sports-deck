SECTION 02  ·  PILLAR 03  ·  MUTUAL ACCOUNTABILITY

THE TWO-SIDED
CONTRACT.

Every party has a vested interest. Two columns, equal weight. This is what the PLAYERS Academy puts in writing — what students sign, and what employers sign.
STUDENT COMMITS TO

The PLAYER.

  • Complete core classes before placement
  • Show up — attendance is non-negotiable
  • Submit weekly check-ins to the Academy
  • Complete assigned deliverables on schedule
  • Attend the mid-semester review meeting
  • Present capstone at end-of-term Showcase
  • Maintain professional conduct at all times
EMPLOYER COMMITS TO

The PARTNER.

  • Pay interns a defined hourly wage
  • Provide a named supervisor of record
  • Define role responsibilities in writing
  • Hold a structured onboarding session
  • Complete the mid-semester evaluation form
  • Attend the end-of-term Showcase event
  • Submit a post-placement feedback survey
MUTUAL ACCOUNTABILITY  ·  SIGNED. BOTH SIDES.

STUDENT VOICES  ·  SHAPING THE PROGRAM FROM THE INSIDE
DISCUSSION  ·  FOUR THINGS WE WANT TO SOLVE

THINGS TO SOLVE.

The PLAYERS Academy is real — but four open questions still shape how we operationalize it. We want your perspective on each.
01
Student Compensation & Credit
The model calls for paid internships, with the financial transaction kept between student and employer (not routed through the program). Students earn course credit. Is that workable within university policy — and how do we reconcile the "paid" commitment with project-based engagements that may not carry compensation?
02
Projects vs. Internships
How should structured projects (e.g. Utah Baseball analytics) be classified and managed within the Academy? Do they follow the same accountability framework as internships, and how do credit, compensation, and employer expectations differ?
03
Operational Roles & Responsibilities
What does Career Services' day-to-day involvement look like? What's the realistic staffing and workflow required to run placements, manage employer relationships, and track outcomes — especially given a Fall 2027 launch? When should a leader be activated?
04
Coordination With Career Success
All positions should likely be posted through U Career Success for institutional alignment. But the Academy's value proposition to employers is a curated candidate pipeline — not a public job board. How do we preserve that curation while working within the university's existing career infrastructure?
